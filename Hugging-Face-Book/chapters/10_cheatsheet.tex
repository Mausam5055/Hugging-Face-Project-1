\chapter{Terminology Cheat Sheet}

A quick reference for common AI and Hugging Face terms.

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.5}
\begin{tabular}{|p{0.3\textwidth}|p{0.6\textwidth}|}
\hline
\rowcolor{hfblue!20} \textbf{Term} & \textbf{Simple Explanation} \\ \hline
\textbf{Model} & The AI program (the brain) that makes predictions. \\ \hline
\textbf{Weights} & The learned numbers inside the model (its memory). \\ \hline
\textbf{Checkpoint} & A saved version of the model during training. \\ \hline
\textbf{BOS / EOS} & Special tokens marking the \textbf{B}eginning and \textbf{E}nd \textbf{O}f a \textbf{S}entence. \\ \hline
\textbf{Epoch} & One full cycle of training through the entire dataset. \\ \hline
\textbf{Batch Size} & How many examples the model looks at at once. \\ \hline
\textbf{Learning Rate} & How fast the model learns (too fast = simple mistakes, too slow = takes forever). \\ \hline
\textbf{Loss} & The error score (lower is better). \\ \hline
\textbf{Fine-tuning} & Tweaking a pre-trained model for your specific data. \\ \hline
\textbf{Zero-shot} & Using a model to do a task it wasn't explicitly trained for. \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
